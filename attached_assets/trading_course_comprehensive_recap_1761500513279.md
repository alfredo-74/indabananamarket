## MODULE 2: RAW DATA FUNDAMENTALS

### 2.1 - Raw Data Introduction
**Core Concept**: Understanding what drives price movement behind the charts

#### Key Points:
- **Professional Trading Environment Simulation**: This module simulates the first weeks in a professional trading firm
- **Your Job as a Trader**: Simply to predict price movements
- **Real-World Analogies**: Trading is no different from predicting prices in any business:
  - Personal training fees
  - Hairdressing fees  
  - Legal fees
  - Mechanics costs
- **Supply and Demand Factors**: What drives all prices
  - **Supply**: Availability of buyers/sellers (intention)
  - **Demand**: Actual aggressive buying/selling (aggression)
- **Key Distinction**: 
  - Intention = Orders sitting on bid/ask (only visible in DOM)
  - Aggression = Actual transactions taking place (visible in Time & Sales)

#### Important Timeline:
- Professional firms: 6-12 months before trading real money
- Recommended minimum: 3 months studying DOM
- Ideal timeframe: 6 months of observation

### 2.2 - Raw Data: Time & Sales
**Core Concept**: Understanding the purest form of exchange data

#### Components of Time & Sales:
1. **Timestamp**: When transaction occurred
2. **Price**: Transaction price level
3. **Volume**: Number of contracts traded
4. **Type**: Ask (buy) or Bid (sell)

#### Key Learning:
- Time & Sales shows ONLY aggressive transactions (market orders)
- Does NOT show passive orders (limit orders waiting)
- Futures market used as proxy for spot FX market
- Block orders are split by exchange algorithm (CME)

#### Platform Comparison:
- **Retail Platform**: Shows only bid/ask (2 pieces of info)
- **Professional Platform**: Shows additional 4 columns:
  - Aggressive buying volume
  - Aggressive selling volume
  - Passive bid depth
  - Passive offer depth

### 2.3 - Raw Data: Time & Sales Live Example
**Practical Application**: Real-time market observation

#### What to Watch For:
- Transaction flow patterns
- Size of aggressive orders
- Speed of transactions
- Relationship between aggression and price movement

### 2.4 - Raw Data Conclusion
**Summary and Transition Points**

#### Critical Takeaways:
- Time & Sales = Raw data in purest format
- Professional firms ignore charts initially (first 1-2 months)
- Market decisions made in seconds, not with indicators
- Institutional players making snap decisions visible in order flow

#### Differences to Understand:
- **Spot vs Futures**: Why professionals use futures as proxy
- **Why Charts are Secondary**: Everything happens in the order flow first
- **Dynamic Decision Making**: 
  - Example: 51-53 contracts buying during downmove
  - Instant thesis change based on subsequent selling
  - Shows scalper getting "run over" vs profit-taking
